Poly Hanoi Project

A Cal Poly SLO Project

About our Project

Started in 20XX and Led by Javier Gonzalez Sanchez & Rafael Antonio Guerra Silva

The Poly Hanoi Project focuses on the integration of advanced robotics with human workforces to create seamless and efficient collaboration between humans and robots. Our project aims to enhance productivity and safety in various industries by leveraging the strengths of both human intelligence and robotic precision.

Our team at Cal Poly SLO is dedicated to researching and developing innovative solutions that facilitate smooth interaction between humans and robots. By studying the dynamics of human-robot collaboration, we aim to create systems that are intuitive, adaptable, and capable of working in harmony with human counterparts.

Our project is currently at a stage where we are looking to expand our collaborations and invite partnerships from industries interested in pioneering human-robot collaboration. We believe that the future of work lies in the symbiotic relationship between humans and robots, and we are excited to be at the forefront of this transformation.

We are committed to pushing the boundaries of what is possible in human-robot interaction and are constantly exploring new ways to make our systems more efficient and user-friendly.

Current status: In Progress

How it works

This shows a breakdown of what we're trying to do, and how we are doing it

  • Step 1 — Redefining The Tower of Hanoi

    The Tower of Hanoi is a relatively simple puzzle with simple rules... Unless you're like us and change the rules slightly so that you're only allowed to move the objects with your mind!

  • Step 2 — How do we do it?

    Some might call it cheating, but we're engineers so we call it using our head. Specifically we will use our head with an EEG headset, which detects brain waves, and use code to relay those instructions to a robot arm to solve the puzzle.

  • Step 3 — Let's Break it Down

    break down how we will seperate the steps of the process,"While this may seem simple enough, it's actually a lot of work so we seperate the project into a few basic pieces. 1:camera 2:arm 3:headset 4:algorithms"

  • Step 4 — Let's Think Bigger

    This project represents the initial phase in developing a new method for Human-Robot Interaction (HRI). A collaborative robot (cobot) is designed to work alongside a human to complete a common task. One challenge in Human-Robot Collaboration (HRC) is enabling cobots to recognize a human’s affective and cognitive states, which are crucial for decision-making and overall cognitive ability. This research explores how understanding the user’s affective state, particularly stress and focus, can enhance Human-Robot Collaboration.
